The Heavy Crude Spread Trade

How a Venezuela Disruption Flows Through to Investment Returns

The Chain Reaction

1
Supply Disruption
Venezuela's ~1 mb/d of heavy crude comes offline. 67% of output is heavy sour—exactly what Gulf Coast cokers need.
2
Refiners Scramble
Gulf Coast refiners bid up prices for Canadian WCS, Mexican Maya, and Colombian Castilla—the closest substitutes.
3
Differential Compresses
WCS discount to WTI narrows from -$14 toward par—or flips to a premium. This spread move is the core trade.
4
Producer Windfall
Canadian oil sands producers receive higher realized prices. A $10/bbl improvement = ~$3.6B/yr revenue uplift.

Trading the Spread

The Key Relationship
WCS = WTI + Differential
WCS futures trade as a spread to WTI—not an absolute price. You're betting purely on heavy vs. light crude scarcity.
